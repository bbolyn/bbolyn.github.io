Dear Data

"Dear Data" is a project where two designers, Giorgia Lupi and Stefanie Posavec, sent each other postcards every week for a year. Each postcard had a hand-drawn data visualization based on things from their daily lives. This project showed how personal data can be turned into art. Learn more about the project here.

For this assignment, I explored the "Dear Data" website, looked at the different visualizations, and made my own postcard based on my own data. I tracked how frequently I drank anything and for what reason I drank it.

Text Analysis: The Hound of the Baskervilles

This project used digital humanities tools to analyze the first two chapters of Arthur Conan Doyle’s The Hound of the Baskervilles. I marked up the text using XML to identify key characters, objects, and concepts. Then I used Voyant Tools to look at trends across a larger corpus of mystery novels.

Encoding the text helped me understand how structure and repetition work in storytelling, while Voyant revealed patterns in word frequency and theme that I wouldn’t have noticed otherwise.

AI Project: SarcastiBot

For this project, I edited the original Eliza chatbot JavaScript to create something completely different. I made a rude, sarcastic AI with no intention of being helpful. It's a satire of artificial intelligence and its often overly-polite tone. All of the chatbot's response rules were rewritten to be negative and a dry comedic tone.

Game Making Project: Toxic Gym

This Twine game explores gym culture and social media pressure with humor and interactive storytelling. Players make decisions to gain clout, credibility, or just survive the chaos of “The Pump Palace.” Built using Twine, HTML, and CSS, with custom images generated using AI.
