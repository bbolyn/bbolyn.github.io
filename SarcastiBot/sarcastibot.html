Talk to SarcastiBot

This is a sarcastic version of the classic ELIZA chatbot. Don't expect helpful responses—SarcastiBot is here to mock your thoughts, not explore them.



Project Reflection

For this project, I transformed the original ELIZA chatbot into something entirely different—a sarcastic, negative AI that delivers dry humor instead of emotional support. My goal was to explore how tone and language can change the entire vibe of an AI interaction without actually changing its structure or core functionality. SarcastiBot doesn’t try to help you—it roasts you. And that's the point.

I edited the elizadata.js file to rewrite nearly every category of response. For example, where the original might say “Tell me more about that,” my version might say “Wow, groundbreaking. You and everyone else feel that way.” I focused especially on replacing fallback responses and emotional keywords to create the illusion that the bot is tired, judgmental, or completely uninterested. That tone gave the bot a strong personality, which made it more fun to test and refine.

Beyond the sarcasm, this project helped me better understand how chatbots actually work. I saw how simple keyword patterns and reassembly rules can produce fluid conversations, and how matching user input with sarcastic twists makes even canned responses feel dynamic. I also learned how JavaScript connects logic to the interface and how a chatbot’s memory works across inputs.

Overall, this was a fun way to get more comfortable editing JavaScript while also thinking creatively about how AI can be shaped by personality and voice. SarcastiBot may not be helpful, but it definitely leaves an impression.
